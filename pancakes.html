Ingredients


  • 1 large egg per person
  • All purpose flour, the amount is the same weight as the egg
  • Whole Milk, again same weight as the egg
  • 1/2 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 tsp sugar (or salt for savoury pancakes)
  • 1 Tbsp real butter

Equipment


  • Weighing scales
  • Large Mixing bowl
  • Non stick frying pan
  • Balloon Whisk
  • Ladle
  • Spatula/fish slice (Silicon or wooden only! Don't scratch that non stick pan!)

Method


  1. Place mixing bowl on weighing scales and set scale to zero
  2. Crack 1 egg per person into the bowl and note the weight
  3. Add in the same weight each of flour and milk
  4. Season with sugar (or salt if making savoury pancakes)
  5. Add in bicarbonate of soda (this makes the pancakes fluffy and can be left out if you prefer)
  6. Whisk ingredients together until the batter is smooth and place in the fridge for 30 mins
  7. Place pan on a med low heat
  8. Add butter to hot pan. Once the butter has stopped foaming it's ready to cook with
  9. Add 1 ladleful of batter at a time to pan. Swirl batter around to coat pan entirely
  10. Once bubbles form on the top of the pancake it's ready to flip (approx 1-2 min)
  11. Flip the pancake with your spatula/fish slice (or do a pan flip if you're brave enough!) and cook for a further minute
  12. Place cooked pancake on a plate and keep warm in the oven while you cook the rest
  13. Serve with whatever you like! Fresh cream, maple syrup, bacon...whatever tastes good to you!
